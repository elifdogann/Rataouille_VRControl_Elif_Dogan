## Bridging Research and Practice

Hello, I’m Elif Doğan. I am a Computer Science master’s student based in Darmstadt, and I work on automation and software development with a strong interest in building practical, high-impact systems. My academic path and professional experience have developed together in a way that constantly feeds both sides: while my studies deepen my understanding of computer science, my hands-on work keeps me focused on building solutions that are useful, measurable, and grounded in real needs. My background combines graduate-level computer science with earlier training in industrial engineering, which has shaped the way I approach technical problems from both a systems and process perspective.

## From Data-Oriented Work to Automation Design

Over time, my work has moved across data science, analytics, reporting, and software development, but the common thread has always been the same: identifying complexity, understanding structure, and turning repeated or difficult processes into clearer systems. I have worked on analytical and artificial intelligence projects, customer-oriented reporting, data structure analysis, and later on Python-based applications that automate core accounting processes, reduce manual workload, and improve data exchange between internal tools and external platforms. That progression shaped the way I think about engineering today. I am especially interested in solutions that sit between technical depth and practical value—systems that do not only function correctly, but also improve the way people work.

## Building with Structure and Intent

What I enjoy most about software development is not only implementation, but the process of structuring a problem. I am particularly drawn to automation, statistical and exploratory analysis, visualization, and software that connects data with actionable workflows. In practice, this means working with tools and technologies such as Python, SQL, Streamlit, PySide6, cloud services, machine learning libraries, and data visualization frameworks, while keeping the design goal clear from the beginning. I try to approach each project with the same question: how can a technical system become more understandable, more maintainable, and more useful for the people who interact with it?

## A Technical Mindset with a Human Focus

I see technology as both an engineering discipline and a form of communication. A good system should not only solve a problem internally; it should also make its logic readable, scalable, and adaptable. This is one of the reasons I enjoy working across areas such as automation, data-driven decision support, applied AI, and interactive software. Alongside technical work, I have also been involved in mentoring, volunteer teaching, and community-based learning environments, which strengthened my belief that technical knowledge becomes more meaningful when it is shared and made accessible. That perspective continues to shape the way I build and document projects.
